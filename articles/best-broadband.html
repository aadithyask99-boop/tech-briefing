Best Broadband Deals in 2026: Full Fibre Compared

By Tech Briefing Editorial · 9 June 2026

Full fibre broadband — where fibre optic cable runs all the way to the premises — is now available to over 60% of UK homes, up from just 10% four years ago. The rollout has intensified competition among providers, driving down prices significantly.

Openreach's wholesale network underpins most UK broadband providers, including BT, Sky, TalkTalk, and Vodafone. Virgin Media operates its own independent cable network covering around 55% of UK homes, while alternative network operators such as CityFibre, Hyperoptic, and Toob are building independent full-fibre infrastructure in specific areas.

Download speeds for full fibre packages range from 100Mbps to 1Gbps, with most household users finding 500Mbps more than sufficient for streaming, gaming, and working from home simultaneously.

Contract terms are typically 18 or 24 months, with introductory pricing that rises at renewal. The real cost of a broadband package is the average monthly price over the full contract period, including any setup fees and router delivery charges.

For homes not yet covered by full fibre, FTTC (fibre to the cabinet) superfast broadband offering 30-80Mbps remains the most widely available option. ADSL connections delivering under 20Mbps are increasingly rare as network upgrades continue.
