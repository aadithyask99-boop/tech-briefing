Best Antivirus Software for 2026: UK Buyer's Guide

By Tech Briefing Editorial · 8 June 2026

The antivirus software market has consolidated significantly, with Norton, Bitdefender, Kaspersky, ESET, and Malwarebytes accounting for the majority of consumer installations. Detection rates across leading products have converged — top-tier products now detect 99.5% or more of known malware samples.

Real-time protection, behavioural analysis, and cloud-based threat intelligence are standard across all premium tiers. Ransomware protection has become a key selling point, given the volume and sophistication of attacks targeting individuals and small businesses.

For most home users, Windows Defender provides adequate baseline protection at no additional cost. Paid products justify their pricing through reduced false positives, lighter system footprint, and supplementary tools such as VPNs, password managers, and dark web monitoring.

Subscription pricing typically ranges from £20 to £80 per year for single-device cover, with multi-device household licences offering better value for families. Annual renewals often carry steep price increases — shopping for a new subscription is frequently cheaper than accepting the auto-renewal rate.

When evaluating antivirus software, prioritise products with independently verified no-logs VPN policies, minimal performance impact on older hardware, and transparent privacy policies regarding telemetry data collection.
