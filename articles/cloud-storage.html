Cloud Storage Comparison 2026: Which Service Is Best?

By Tech Briefing Editorial · 7 June 2026

Cloud storage has become essential infrastructure for most people who use smartphones and computers. The market is dominated by four providers: Google Drive, Microsoft OneDrive, Apple iCloud, and Dropbox, each with distinct strengths depending on the user's existing ecosystem.

Free tier allocations vary considerably. Google Drive offers 15GB shared across Gmail, Drive, and Photos. Microsoft OneDrive gives 5GB free, with 100GB for £1.99 per month. Apple iCloud provides 5GB free, with 50GB for 99p per month. Dropbox has reduced its free tier to 2GB, making it the least competitive at entry level.

For users already in a particular ecosystem, the native service is usually the rational choice. Microsoft 365 Personal includes 1TB of OneDrive storage alongside Office apps — extremely cost-competitive if Office is already needed.

Dropbox differentiates on collaboration features, selective sync granularity, and third-party integrations. Its tight Slack, Zoom, and Adobe integrations make it popular in professional environments, though pricing is harder to justify for individual consumers.

Security and privacy considerations are increasingly relevant. End-to-end encrypted alternatives such as Proton Drive and Tresorit offer stronger privacy guarantees but with fewer collaboration features and higher prices.
